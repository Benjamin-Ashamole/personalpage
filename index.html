Chemist

I fell in love with chemistry after taking it for the first time in the 10th grade, and I haven’t looked back ever since. Learning how particles interact at the molecular level has been enlightening beyond my wildest imaginations. Two things that truly makes chemistry special is that chemistry reconciles the other two core sciences (Physics & Biology) and it is a breeding ground for innovation. Over the years, I have learnt, practiced and become proficient in some of its techniques such as High Resolution NMR, FTIR, Mass Spectroscopy, Protein Expression & Purification, Organic Synthesis, and Computation. Major projects that I have worked on consisted of synthesizing twelve novel polycationic amphiphiles and then investigating their antimicrobial and colloidal characteristics. Also, Investigating the binding kinetics between Ubiquitin protein and Yeast Ubiquitin Hydrolase (YUH1), using NMR, Molecular Dynamic, Isothermal Titration Calorimetry etc. I received my B.S in Biophysical Chemistry from James Madison University, and I am currently a Ph.D chemistry candidate at Rutgers University in New Jersey.

Developer

After graduation from my undergraduate studies at James Madison University, I decided to take a gap year in order to explore other interests, broaden my skill set and thus make myself more marketable in the ever-growing but fiercely competitive job market that our generation has conjured so I decided to learn how to develop internet based products. I started teaching myself how to program using free online resources such as Codecademy, FreeCodeCamp, Shaw Academy as well as going to meetup groups and chatting with actual programmers. After 8 months, I had reached a point where I felt comfortable, and decided to up the challenge. I attended Make School’s summer academy in San Francisco where I was taught not just how to write clean code, but also how to efficiently create and see a project through to the end.
